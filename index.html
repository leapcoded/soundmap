How to use
Quick start

1. Set scale & map — use the px/m slider to match your site map. Upload a map image, or click Satellite to search for a location and automatically sync the scale to the map zoom.

2. Place speakers — select the Speaker tool and click the canvas. Set SPL, beam angle, bass bias, and mount height in the inspector.

3. Add zones — drag zone shapes to define areas (Stage, Camping, Dancefloor…). The schedule panel shows estimated SPL at each zone centre.

4. Add barriers — Heras panels and hay bales block and absorb sound. Place between sources and sensitive areas; angle for best effect.

5. Check frequencies — switch to Low (125 Hz) to see bass propagation. This is usually the noise-compliance concern for camping and residential areas.

6. Export — fill in the event details in the sidebar, then export a clean PNG containing the heatmap, a trimmed satellite backdrop, and the full SPL table.

Tools

Speaker — click to place. Peak SPL sets the source level; dispersion angle narrows or widens the coverage cone; bass bias shifts spectral energy towards low or high frequencies; mount height affects diffraction over barriers.

Hay Bales & Heras — absorptive barriers. Effective at mid/high frequencies, limited at bass. Works best angled as a baffle rather than a straight wall — use the Rotation slider to adjust.

Zone palette — areas for the SPL schedule. Canopy zones (stretch tents) are acoustically transparent and are excluded from the SPL table. Speakers under a canopy show a dashed indicator ring.

Custom Zones — use the Custom tool to draw any element. Toggle 'Exclusion zone' in the inspector to block it out with a checkerboard pattern, or use the colour picker for visual organisation.

Ruler — click and drag to measure any distance on the canvas. Reading shown in metres.

Acoustic model & display

Scale — set this before placing anything. All distance and SPL calculations derive from it; changing it later does not rescale existing items.

Satellite & Veil — when using the satellite view, use the 'Map veil' slider to dim the background map and make the acoustic heatmap easier to read.

Ground type — grass attenuates mid/high frequencies more than hard standing (ground absorbs the reflected wave). Concrete adds a ~3 dB boost from constructive ground reflection.

Barriers — height dominates. A 4 m barrier gives roughly 9 dB insertion loss at 125 Hz and 23 dB at 4 kHz (Maekawa formula). Use the Reflection slider to model barrier solidity; Absorption to model transmission loss through thin or porous surfaces.

Wind — boosts SPL downwind and attenuates it upwind, scaled by distance. Set speed and bearing to model prevailing conditions during the event.

Festival planning tips

Set scale before placing anything — it cannot be changed without manually repositioning all items.

Upload your site map and lower the Map Opacity slider until the heatmap colours read clearly through it.

Place speakers first, then add zones to see SPL readings in context. The schedule panel updates in real time as you drag elements.

Low band (125 Hz) is almost always the compliance concern. Check this view for camping areas and residential boundaries near the site.

Save the project JSON at the end of each session — it preserves all placements, settings, and event details for the next review or handoff.
